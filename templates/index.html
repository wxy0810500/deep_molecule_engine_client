Model Documentation
Fig 1. ADMET properties with abbreviation covered in this service

ADMET, i.e. Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, Excretion and Toxicity, is one of the import domains in drug research & development. Candidates have to be systematically optimized by balancing multiple key ADMET properties. Computational predicting ADMET properties makes it possible to assess ADMET properties virtually and early in drug pipelines, which will help to speed up the optimization process by better experimental design and saving resources and time.

Combining different drug pipeline knowledge, about 50 important and common ADMET properties (listed in Fig 1) were constructed with deep learning predictive models in GHDDI’s free ADMET prediction service. For each ADMET property, positive, toxic or active compounds were labeled 1, while negative, nontoxic or inactive ones were labeled 0. Based on the comprehensive historic experimental data, 61 predictive models were constructed and validated in 5-fold cross validation. Performance was measured using AUC, AuPR, precision, recall, and F1. All the models in this service will be maintained and updated regularly.

This service helps to automatically profile and systematically assess your own compounds’ ADMET properties through a submit workflow. The report ADMET radar chart is generated by average different properties in this type after standardization. For each end of radar graph, the higher the score, the better or more drug-like the compound is. Each submit can handle 100 compounds at most.
